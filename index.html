Subway Surfers

by: Joaquin Pruneda Paz

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I wanted to make an infinite game where the only goal is to get the highest score possible. My first thought was Temple Run or Subway Surfers. As a kid, I would play temple runs and subway Surfers with my family all the time, so this game brought good memories for me. So I started my game with haste

2. What I Learned

I had accidentally not saved my game before sending it to my peers. I had commented out the boundaries and left the collision boxes marked. However, the restart button did not work as I intended it to and the instructions were too vague.

3. Architecture of My Game

I have a class for the cars, the trains, the coins, and the character. They all interact with each other in order to make sure that the collision boxes work properly. Also, the Game Panel is where most of my game is being coded to make sure that the game switches windows and the controls work.

4. Technical Implementation

I am proud of the collision boxes on my game. I had to set the collision boxes in each of my characters and obstacles. Then I had to make sure that when the players hit each other they die or get collected. This took many trials because of all the simple mistakes that were made.

5. Most Challenging Part

I was stuck multiple times in parts of coding where the answer seemed simple. It took tons of classes to finally perfect this game, but I am finally happy with the result except for a few tweaks that I could do in the future.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would like to make the player movements smoother and I would like to make it so that the cars and trains move faster as time goes on.
